About

A filmmaker rooted in both worlds

Percival Li is a China-born filmmaker and Film & Television student at Columbia College Chicago, graduating in 2026. His training spans directing, producing, cinematography, lighting, editing, and screenwriting — with hands-on experience across professional camera systems, lighting setups, and industry-standard post-production software.

His most ambitious project, Conspiracy — a short film he directed, wrote, and produced — stands out for its strong visual identity and narrative execution, leading a crew of 50. As producer on The Cycle, he managed full production logistics and cross-departmental coordination. Most recently, he helmed The Quiet Between Us as director, producer, writer, and editor — from development through post.

Growing up in Shenzhen, China and studying filmmaking in the United States has shaped a perspective that blends patience and visual atmosphere with structure and narrative clarity — resulting in films that feel both culturally grounded and broadly resonant.

Creative Vision & Theme

Light, space, and what lies between

Visual Storytelling

Rather than relying on dialogue alone, the creative approach emphasizes atmosphere, rhythm, and composition to communicate feeling. The way light falls across a room, the silence between characters, the pacing of movement within a scene — these details often reveal more about human experience than words alone.

Cross-Cultural Perspective

Growing up in Shenzhen and studying in Chicago has shaped a lens that blends Chinese storytelling traditions — observation, patience, atmosphere — with American filmmaking's focus on structure and narrative momentum. The result is cinema that is both visually thoughtful and emotionally direct.

"Small details — the way light falls across a room, the silence between characters — can reveal more about human experience than words alone."

Professional Goals

Where I'm headed

Short-term · Post-Graduation

Join a professional production team

After graduation, the immediate goal is to work within an established film or media production company — gaining real-world experience in lighting, camera operation, scheduling, budgeting, and team coordination. Learning from experienced professionals and building industry connections is the foundation of a lasting career.

Mid-term · Growing Craft

Develop as a storyteller

Beyond technical skills, the deeper goal is to continue evolving as a storyteller. Work on productions across different departments — directing, producing, camera — has built an understanding of how each role shapes the final result. That cross-disciplinary knowledge directly informs stronger, more intentional filmmaking.

Long-term · The Bigger Picture

Establish an independent film studio

The long-term vision is to found an independent production company — creating projects that combine strong visual storytelling with meaningful themes. As someone who grew up in Shenzhen and studied in Chicago, the goal is to produce films that reflect perspectives from different cultures. Cinema has the power to connect people across backgrounds, and this work aims to contribute to that exchange. The studio would be a space where craft, collaboration, and cultural honesty come first.
